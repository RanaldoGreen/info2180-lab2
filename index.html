One Piece

July 16, 2022 by Aiden

One piece is a manga/anime series that is written and illustrated by Eiichiro Oda. One Piece was first published in 1997 with the anime being aired 2 years later in 1999. It is one of the longest-running manga and anime series, with over 1000 chapters in manga and over 1000 episodes in the anime as well. It is known as one of the "Big Three Manga" along Bleach and Naruto. This was a term used to describe the three most popular running series during their golden age in Shonen-Jump. Everyone loves One Piece because of its engaging storyline and a huge set of character development.

Due to its popularity and the love of its fans, One Piece sold around 321 million manga from 1997 to 2014. This landed it, in the Guinness Book Of World Records for the most copies that were published for the same comic book series by a single author. Fast forward 8 years later, the manga now has officially sold over 500,000 copies as of the beginning of August 2022. The Officil Guiness World Record: "The most copies published for the same comic book series by a single author", certified to Eiichiro Oda in 2015, has now been renewed. As such Oda Sensei has been awarded a new official certificate.

Rottweiler

September 07, 2022 by Chloe

Although there is no documentation of the Rottweiler's origins, most people think the breed descended from the herding dogs of ancient Rome. Throughout their long history, they've been used to perform many different jobs. In the past when the Roman empire was expanding, it needed a way of feeding massive traveling armies of men. They used their dogs to herd livestock and march them along with the army. This was the only viable way for the soldiers to have a steady food supply without refrigeration. Today the Rottweiler participants in herding events, and can keep up with the sheepdogs and shepherds in the field.

By the mid-1800s, the Rottweiler had fallen out of favor. Dog carts had been replaced by donkey carts and then railroads. Cattle droving was outlawed, leaving Rottweilers with no work. However the breed bounced back in the early 1900s when it gained popularity as a police dog. When World War I hit in 1914, Rottweilers served with German police officers. Becoming the fourth dog breed to be officially recognized as a police dog. Around this same time, Rotts came to be recognized as official military dogs. This opened up other doors for Rotties in working positions. They even became one of the first dog breeds to be trained as guide dogs for visually impaired people.
